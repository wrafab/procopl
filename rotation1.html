• 3D Printed Wax Stamp


    Fleur De Lis Design




    My final design in the Fusion 360 program

            Using 2d shapes, extrusions, filets, and other tools within Fusion 360, I designed this ornate stamp.  
        After getting the hang of the program, this took probably 20 minutes to create.  
        To achieve the "P" and the Fleur De Lis on the bottom (as seen in the last picture below), I imported photos, 
        traced around them with line segments, then extruded those 2D shapes into what they are now.

    My imported stl file in Makerbot Print

            After exporting my design as a stl file, I opened it in Makerbot Print. I first changed the units to
        inches, flipped it, added supports, and then exported it onto a flashdrive.  I started the printed process on one of the 5th gen
        Makerbots and it took roughly 2.5 hours to finish.

    My printed and sanded stamp

            This is the final product! It came out great, especially the "P" and Fleur de Lis.  I removed all the supports, extra plastic
        and then sanded it down so it looks as sleek as a 3D printed object can. I'm happy with how it turned out.

    The bottom of the stamp--a fleur de lis shown

            This is the functional part of the stamp, the actual stamp part.  It both printed very precicely since I positioned it upwards in
        the printing process and it works too--I tested it.
